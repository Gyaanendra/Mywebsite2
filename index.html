Some Interesting Facts about Camel

There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms. Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in. There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.

Some Interesting Facts about chameleon

THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS. ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR.

CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE. THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE. SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL. UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN’T REGROW THEIR TAILS.

Some Interesting Facts about Panda

Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day! A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day! Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit. Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago. Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.

Some Interesting Facts about teddy bear

Before teddy bears could be sold, Michtom had to get President Roosevelt’s permission to use his name. Which he did and so the era of Teddy’s bear began! You can get teddy bears that are made from alpaca pelt today. This kind of teddy bear is going to be very soft and expensive! There is a dedicated magazine to teddy bears. There are about 2,000 speciality bear making stores in America! This does not count stores like Build-A-Bear.
